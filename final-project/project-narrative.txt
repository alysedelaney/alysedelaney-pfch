How Sad are the Sad Girls? A Lyric Analysis of Indie Music by Womxn and Non-Binary Artists 
----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

-noticing a rise in headlines such as "Crisis in American Girlhood" (Washington Post), "How Quickly Can a Girl go Viral on TikTok?" (Atlantic), "The Joy of Sad Girl Music" (Harper's Bazaar), recent dicussions about a mental health crisis in teenage girls, alonside music i've been listening to in which related themes emerge, the rise of "sad girl music" notably marked by the trio boygenius, also with mass fandom (teenage girls passing out at Phoebe Bridgers), young female performers such as clairo, beabadoobee, and mxmtoon rising to fame via "bedroom pop" 

Noticing themes of love (as is the eternal human condition of songwriting), but also coming of age, sexuality and queerness, identity, mental health struggles
Seems to be spurred on by the pandemic

*Are the lyrics and themes young female, femme, non-binary musicians are writing about currently reflect the trending discussion? 

STEP 1: BUILD A LIST OF ARTISTS ===============================================================

From my personal listening habits, I had a general notion about the artists I wanted to focus on, but wanted to dive more into additional research on the genre, to make sure I wasn't limiting my analysis to my personal music tastes/experience. To start, I needed to compile a list of artists to be included in my analysis. Main criteria - womxn and non-binary artists; young (30 and under, preferably even under 25); fit within or in proximity to general "indie" pop music, focusing on indie pop and bedroom pop but can be from adjacent genres. Bedroom Pop particularly because of the DIY. Also wanted to capture artists who have acheived a certain level of fame, superstardom, virality in recent years. With millions of followers, something they are singing about must be resonating with listeners. 

Things to Note: 
1- Classifying music is hard
2- Saying something belongs to a genre is different than saying something sounds like a genre (that youtube video)
3- A note on navigating gender identity
